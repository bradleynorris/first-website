Backpacking

To get to fire lookouts, you may get lucky and have an open road all the way there. More likey, you will need to traverse to them by foot. Depending on the distance and your personal preference, backpacking can be a great way to experience your first fire lookout. Backpacking essentially consists of hiking to a location and staying the night; carrying all the gear you will need on your back. Common items needed can be found listed to the right. When out in the wild, it is important to make sure you have a plan. Detailed topographical maps, a compass or modern GPS can be your best friend in the wild.

Hazards on the Trail

There are some hazards on the trail that are easily avoidable, such as lack of water sources. Other hazards are not quite as easy to plan for. Bears are a real threat in the high mountains and should be taken seriously at all times. I know from experience. Hopefully you never have an encounter with a bear, but just in case, it is important to know what to do.

Essential Gear

There are some basic items you need to start your backpacking adventures. The first is a backpack to put it all in. Next, you'll need a tent and a sleeping; bag both of which vary by size and seasonal effectiveness. A water purification system is key for staying hydrated on the trail. A headlamp is key for seeing at night. A mattress pad will help you sleep comfortably. Lastly, you'll need clothing and a supply of food.
