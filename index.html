FIRE LOOKOUTS

So. What's a Fire Lookout?

Fire Lookouts have been used for years as a way of monitoring wilderness areas for fire activity. Fire Lookouts are placed strategically on high peaks to be able to view long distances and keep an eye out for fires.

What Drew Me To Fire Lookouts

I am an avid backpacker and hiker. I always enjoy getting out into the wilderness and finding new adventures. When I learned about Fire Lookouts from a Reddit post, I knew I needed to see them first-hand.

Who's There?

Due to advancements in satellite technology, the need for fire lookouts to be permanently occupied has been drastically reduced. Some lookouts have since been abandoned and some continue to be staffed only in the spring and summer months. Alternatively, some lookouts have been converted to rental units, but space is limited and reservations sell out quickly.

Towers Nearby

It turns out that in Fresno County alone, there are 12 documented Fire Lookout locations. Some are fully operational, some are out of service and some were never actually built; just a concrete slab on a mountain. You can see the full list of California Fire Lookouts here.
